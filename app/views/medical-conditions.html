Do you have any medical conditions that affect your ability to drive?

You must declare any medical condition that could affect your driving. This is a legal requirement. Failing to declare a relevant condition is a criminal offence and can result in a fine of up to £1,000.

Conditions you may need to declare include:

  • epilepsy or seizures
  • certain heart conditions
  • diabetes treated with insulin or certain medication
  • significant visual impairment
  • sleep apnoea
  • mental health conditions
  • neurological conditions such as Parkinson's disease or multiple sclerosis
